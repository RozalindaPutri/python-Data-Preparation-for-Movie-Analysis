## 🎬 Movie Analytics & Profit Prediction using Python

📌 **Project Overview**
This project focuses on analyzing movie performance using data analytics and machine learning techniques. The objective is to understand what factors influence a movie’s financial success and audience reception.
The dataset consists of movie metadata such as budget, revenue, popularity, and ratings. The data is cleaned through preprocessing steps including handling missing values, removing duplicates, and preparing relevant features.A new variable called **profit (revenue – budget)** is created through feature engineering to better represent financial performance. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is then conducted to identify patterns in profit distribution, rating behavior, and key influencing factors.
In addition, classification models such as **K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN)** and **Decision Tree** are applied to predict whether a movie is successful based on its features. Model performance is compared to determine the most suitable approach.

🎯 **Objectives**
* Analyze movie financial performance using profit metrics
* Identify factors influencing movie success (budget, popularity, rating)
* Explore audience rating patterns
* Build classification models to predict movie success
* Compare KNN and Decision Tree model performance
* Provide data-driven insights for decision-making

📊 **Key Metrics Analyzed**
* Profit distribution (revenue – budget)
* Movie rating distribution
* Budget vs profit relationship
* Popularity impact on success
* Model accuracy comparison (KNN vs Decision Tree)
* Feature influence on prediction

🔍 **Key Insights**
* The movie industry is highly skewed, with only a few films generating very high profits
* Most movies produce low to moderate profit, indicating high financial risk
* Movie duration does not significantly impact financial success
* Ratings are mostly concentrated between 3–4, meaning most movies are moderately well-received
* Budget, popularity, and rating are key drivers of success
* KNN performs better than Decision Tree, indicating similarity-based patterns are more effective
